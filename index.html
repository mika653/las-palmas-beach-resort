Slow Down. Breathe In.
Stay A While.

There's a kind of quiet you can only find on an island like this. The unhurried rhythm of waves rolling onto shore. The warmth of sand beneath your feet as the afternoon stretches on.

The sound of your children laughing as they chase the tide — and the deep, rare pleasure of having nowhere else to be.

Las Palmas — "The Palms" — is named for the coconut groves that shelter our shore in Barangay Kalubakis. Far from the rush of the mainland, this is a place that remembers what rest feels like. Come for a weekend. Stay until the island tells you it's time to go.

Getting Here

Finding Your Way
to Paradise

The journey is part of the escape.

Travel to Real, Quezon

From Manila, take a bus or drive east to Real, Quezon. The road winds through the Sierra Madre — about 3 to 4 hours, depending on traffic and how many times you stop to admire the mountains.

Catch a Boat from Ungos Port

Regular boats depart for Polillo Island from Ungos Port. The crossing takes around 2 to 3 hours — watch the mainland shrink behind you as the island draws near.

Arrive at Polillo Island

Make your way to Barangay Kalubakis. We'll be here — waiting under the palms, just as we always are.

Need help planning your trip? Reach out and we'll guide you every step of the way.
